About

I am a researcher with a background in statistics, currently focusing on machine learning and public health data science. My research explores how environmental, socioeconomic, and biological factors interact to influence disease dynamics.

I have worked on multiple large-scale datasets involving infectious diseases such as dengue, tuberculosis, malaria, and mortality patterns across different regions.

My work emphasizes interpretable machine learning so that models are not only accurate but also understandable and useful for real-world decision-making.
